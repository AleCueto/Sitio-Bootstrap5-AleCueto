INTRODUCTION

The computer mouse is a device that allows us to communicate with computer equipment, moving a cursor on the screen to point and click, it is a device that is usually connected by USB, although formerly it was connected to the PS / 2 port. It is a lot more important than I think because it has allowed people who are not very computer literate and could not have been able to communicate with a computer with a keyboard and commands to use these tools. What has increased the demand for equipment and therefore the investment of money and the increase in innovation. So thanks to the mouse today the world is what it is.

HISTORY

The first mouse as we know it was designed during the 1960s (1963). It was an invention co-designed by two men, Bill English (right) and Douglas Engelbart (left). This device had the appearance of a wooden paving stone and worked thanks to two metal wheels that established the X and Y axes. It also had a red button at the top. Although it seems very archaic, the logic behind the device is not very different from current mouse.

In 1981 the first PC with a built-in mouse appeared (Xerox star 8010 | right), it also included a graphical user interface Although it already existed, it was with the Mac 1984 that this tool really became popular (left image)

The first commercial mouse were mechanical, that is, they used a plastic ball that moves two wheels, similar to those of the 1963 protocol. Some time later, in 1999, the first optical mouse came out, the Microsoft Intellimouse Intellieye.

Currently the market is focused on "gaming" mouse, dedicated to video games, since this is where a very precise and comfortable pointer is required for competitive games. These have many buttons to allow assigning controls to them, for example for reloading in a shooter video game. Extremely precise and fluid movement has already been achieved in these peripherals and it is difficult to glimpse their future.

There are also new models that provide innovative designs, such as the apple "magic mouse" or the logitech MX with its vertical design.

The mouse models that we know are already highly evolved (polling rate, DPI ...) but new very interesting models are appearing. An example of these are the new devices that allow us to interact in virtual reality environments. Since in these we cannot make use of current devices. It should be noted that these devices would not be mouse as such, they would be more like nice substitutes in VR environments. It is also important to know that these new controllers are at a very early stage, and it is expected that in a few years this technology will be more established, for gaming and even professional functions (architecture, industrial design...).

MOUSE BUYING GUIDE

Comfort.

For this you will have to take into account the shape of the mouse and your type of grip, there are 3 types:

  • Palm: You need a mouse with an inclined curvature to the side with which you are going to hold the mouse.
  • Claw: They are usually elongated and fully centered mouse.
  • Fingers: They are usually smaller mouse with a more round shape, also centered.

The type.

It is important to take into account what is mentioned in the mouse types section:

  • We will have to know if we want a wired or wireless one
  • It is important to know where we will use it to choose between laser (glass table) and infrared (mat or cloth).
  • It is important to know if we want an ergonomic or left-handed one.

The quality.

It is important to take into account the quality of the product:

  • We must ensure that it is made of materials that will not spoil in a short time.
  • We can look at the mouse brand to see if it is of good quality (razer, logitech).
  • We must also adapt this to our needs, we must not fall short or go too far.

Features.

In case you want a mouse for gaming or activities that need speed:

  • DPI or dots per inch, which allows us to see the resolution of the mouse. We will be able to know how many pixels the mouse will move for each inch.
  • IPS or inches per second, which is the speed at which the mouse sensor can accurately track movement.
  • The number of buttons that we want our mouse to have in order to assign keys to them.
